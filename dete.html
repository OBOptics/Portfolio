DETE Exit Survey — Drivers of Turnover

Objective: Understand who is leaving, why, and how tenure and demographics differ across separation types—so HR can target retention and transition programs.

Tools: Tableau Public, Excel Data: 805 exits • Separation type • Tenure • Age • Dissatisfaction

Executive Summary

  • Volume mix: Retirement (347) and Resignation (305) account for most exits.
  • Tenure: Median time served at exit is about 5 years.
  • Dissatisfaction: Overall ~11%, highest for Termination (~20%) and Resignation (~13%).
  • Age pattern: Resignations skew younger vs. retirements, which cluster 50+.

Data Overview

  • Records: 805 exits (DETE survey).
  • Fields: SeparationType, Years Employed, Age, Dissatisfaction indicator, plus demographics.
  • Quality: Cleaned headers; tenure parsed into numeric years; age banded for readability.

Insights & Actions

  • Target resignation hotspots. With dissatisfaction higher in Resignation, pulse-check workloads/leadership where recent resignations concentrate.
  • Retirement pipeline. Retirements are predictable; plan backfills and knowledge-transfer for roles with 50–59 and 60+ clusters.
  • Tenure & onboarding. If early-career exits rise (e.g., <3 years), strengthen onboarding, career pathing, and manager training.
