NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions — Tutorial & Findings

by: Obi Oleru

Download Notebook (.ipynb) Download Dataset (CSV)

This tutorial turns a large NYC crash log into decision-ready insights: when collisions spike, where they cluster across boroughs, and why they happen (top contributing factors), plus a daily time-series decomposition to separate trend from seasonality. The goal is to guide the “3 E’s” of safety: enforcement, engineering, and education.

Outline

  1. Hour-of-Day Profile
  2. Borough Comparison
  3. Top Contributing Factors
  4. Daily Trend & Seasonality (STL)
  5. Hotspots (Map)
  6. Summary & Next Steps

1) Hour-of-Day Profile

We extract Hour of Day from CRASH TIME and aggregate counts. The curve reveals commute peaks and late-night risk windows that inform staffing and messaging.

Commuter peaks (AM/PM) and a late-night shoulder.
Takeaway: Prioritize high-visibility enforcement and variable messaging on the top two risk hours per day.

2) Borough Comparison

We tally crashes by BOROUGH to scope relative burden and budget needs. Combining counts with population/vehicle miles would yield rates, but absolute counts already help target engineering work.

Absolute counts by borough; use rates for equity comparisons.
Takeaway: Focus quick-build safety (signals, daylighting, crossing islands) on the worst corridors within the top borough.

3) Top Contributing Factors

From CONTRIBUTING FACTOR VEHICLE 1 we rank the most cited causes (with “Unspecified” kept but deprioritized). Distraction/inattention often dominates preventable incidents.

Driver inattention/distraction is typically #1—prime target for behavior-change campaigns.
Takeaway: Run geo-targeted PSAs and social ads against distraction; measure pre/post factor share.

4) Daily Trend & Seasonality (STL)

We aggregate to daily counts and apply seasonal decomposition. This separates long-run trend, recurring seasonal patterns, and irregular spikes.

Trend, seasonal, and residual components clarify what’s structural vs transient.
Takeaway: Align staffing to seasonal risk months; treat sudden spikes as incident management, not structural change.

5) Hotspots (Map)

Using LATITUDE/LONGITUDE with folium, we preview hotspots. In the portfolio, include a static screenshot; keep the interactive map inside the notebook.

Static hotspot preview (optional). Use the interactive map in the notebook for exploration.
Takeaway: Prioritize top-density intersections for engineering fixes and targeted enforcement.

Summary & Next Steps

The crash profile is actionable: time-bound enforcement during commute peaks, borough-focused engineering for high-density corridors, and distraction-first education. Seasonality suggests staffing adjustments by month.

  • Immediate: HVE blitz on top 2 hours/day for 4 weeks; track y/y crash change.
  • Engineering: Treat the top 10 intersections by density with quick-build measures; compare injury reduction vs controls.
  • Analytics: Add exposure (traffic volume, weather) for rate-based KPIs; monitor factor-share shifts post-campaign.
