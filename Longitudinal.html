Purpose / Objective

Executive Summary

Opportunity: Structural factors (education, race, gender) interact with psychosocial traits (self-esteem, sociability). Organizations can use these insights to design equitable policies, strengthen workforce development, and tailor wellness programs.

Approach: Best-in-class multivariate and logistic models for the regression tasks, plus one supervised and one unsupervised data-mining method for health and segmentation questions.

Outcome: Education is the strongest income driver; gender and racial gaps persist; self-esteem and sociability matter. AUC≈0.598/Accuracy≈76.2% for sociability classification; decision-tree accuracy is inflated by class imbalance; clustering reveals three distinct sub-groups for targeted programs.

Methodology

Data Overview

Source & scope: National Longitudinal Survey (NLS), ~12,686 participants; demographics, socioeconomic and health variables.

  • Missing values: 37,123 entries — imputed (median for numeric, mode for categoricals).
  • Outliers: >3,000 reviewed; plausible values kept; winsorized at 1st/99th and log-transformed where needed.
  • Subset comparisons: Urban=10,010 vs Non-urban≈2,676; urban mean income ≈ 28,680 vs overall ≈ 28,300; higher variance in urban.
  • Key visual: Positive linear relationship between education and income across races.

Models & Design

Multivariate Regression (Income): Final model with education, parental education, race, gender, self-esteem, and sociability traits.

Logistic Regression (Outgoing Adult): Demographics + gender-role attitudes + self-esteem.

Supervised DM: Classification tree (interpretable) using core demographics and attitudes.

Unsupervised DM: K-means on standardized features to reveal behavioral/economic segments.

Business Impact & Recommendations

  • Invest in education: Scholarships and vocational training raise earnings potential.
  • Address inequities: Equity audits and fair-pay practices to close gender/race gaps.
  • Build soft skills: Mentorship and leadership training to lift self-esteem and sociability.
  • Targeted programs: Use clusters to tailor interventions by segment needs.
